Artifact 04

The Concept
of Forever

I do not know the exact coordinates of our future. But I know the trajectory. If forever is a place, its roads will endlessly loop back to you and me.

I want to build worlds with you. I want to weather the storms and stand quietly in the golden hours of our life together, looking back at the masterpiece we constructed simply by choosing each other.

Fig. 04 — The Eternity

Infinite Tapestry

If I could offer you one impossible gift, it would be my eyes. Just for five minutes. I would want you to step inside my mind and look at yourself the exact way that I look at you. You spend so much time analyzing your perceived flaws, while I spend my time staring at them as if they are the intentional, masterful brushstrokes of a Renaissance painting. There is a geometry to the way you move, a specific cadence to your laugh that I have mapped out in the deepest parts of my memory.

To love you is not a passive event. It is an active, ongoing phenomenon. I love the intricate labyrinth of your thoughts. I love how you feel things with such terrifying depth, and yet you carry yourself with this effortless, floating grace. You are a sanctuary in a world that rarely stops spinning.

Sometimes I think about the sheer mathematical improbability of us. Think about the billions of years of cosmic expansion, the countless civilizations rising and falling, the sheer infinite stretch of human history. Yet, out of all the timelines, out of all the centuries, my timeline collided with yours. We get to exist right now, breathing the same air, under the exact same sky. That is not a coincidence. That is a deliberate act of the universe.

And because of that, my devotion to you is not just a feeling—it is a vow. I do not promise absolute perfection, because human hands are inherently flawed. I do not promise days without rain. But I promise the umbrella. I promise my absolute presence. I promise to be the person who stays, who fights, who listens, and who chooses you over and over again, in every single version of reality, until the stars burn out.
